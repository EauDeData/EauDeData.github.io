Abstract

Orthogonal Subspaces for Date and Content

While time evolves linearly, neural embeddings are chaotic and multi-dimensional. TDIR asks: can we decompose a single embedding into independent temporal and categorical subspaces — so the year of one image can be surgically transplanted onto another, label-free?

Disentangle the space — drag the slider or press play

Entangled TDIR
Car Jacket Poster point size encodes the year (small=1930s, large=1990s)

We propose Temporally Decomposable Image Representations (TDIR) — the first formal treatment of temporal decomposability in visual embeddings. We prove conditions under which decomposition holds, that orthogonality between subspaces emerges from joint optimisation without being explicitly imposed, and characterise the error when conditions are only partially met.

Beyond geometry, TDIR enables transitive operations: the year of one image can be extracted and injected into another with no label supervision — enabling the first zero-label composed historical image retrieval.

Chapter 1 · Introduction

When an Archivist Queries by Composition

Around 5–10% of archival material is photographic. Unlike text, photographs encode temporal information implicitly — in the design of a car, the cut of a jacket, the typography of a poster. Archivists infer dates from objects; TDIR makes this machine-readable.

Probative Value — objects as temporal vessels

Not all objects carry date information equally

In archival science, the probative value of an image is its capacity to uniquely illustrate a phenomenon. Objects evolve at different temporal rhythms. A tire or a pair of shorts are strong temporal signals; a building or a wheel are ambiguous. TDIR learns which objects are reliable "time capsules."

Chapter 2 · Related Work

What Came Before

Prior work observes temporal structure post-hoc, treats object-centricity as a means to date estimation (discarding the object once dated), or requires one encoder per category. TDIR addresses all three gaps with a single unified framework.

DATE ESTIMATION
Palermo et al. (ECCV 12) — low-level cues. Müller et al. (ECIR 17) — DEW regression, no geometric structure. Net et al. (ECIR 24) — 6 categories, one encoder each.
TEMPORAL STRUCTURE (POST-HOC)
Molina et al. (DAS 22) — metric learning re-organisation. Sonthalia et al. (NeurIPS 26) — rankable time in CLIP. Neither induces structure by design.
COMPOSED IMAGE RETRIEVAL
FashionIQ (CVPR 21), Cola (NeurIPS 23) — general domain. EUFCC-CIR (ECCV 24) — cultural heritage, no temporal metadata. No benchmark supports category-date composition.
TDIR (OURS)
First formal treatment of temporal decomposability. Single unified backbone for all 50 categories. First benchmark with object + year annotations. Zero-label composed retrieval.
Chapter 3 · Methodology

Building the Mathematical Foundation

TDIR rests on two definitions, two propositions, a lemma, a theorem, and its corollary — each with a geometric illustration inline. No appendix needed.

§3.1 · Definition 1

A representation is temporally independent from object classes when:

p(µc, c, µy, y) = p(µc, c) · p(µy, y)

Category centroids carry no date information; date centroids carry no category information.

Independence, illustrated — pick a category, then drag the year through it
§3.1 · Definition 2

The year subspace is temporally transitive if for any triple (A, B, C):

∆yA→B + ∆yB→C = ∆yA→C   where  ∆yA→B = µyB − µyA

Necessary for zero-label inference: a year residual from any image acts as a consistent temporal operator when transplanted.

Drag the year proxies — watch the displacement identity hold
Drag µyB — the displacement ΔA→C = ΔA→B + ΔB→C updates live.
§3.2 · Proxy Sets

Three learned proxy sets

The ideal decomposition v = f(x) = µc + µy requires three proxy sets, each with a distinct role at training and inference time.

𝒫c — CATEGORY
µc1 … µcC
One per object category. Integrates over all years → year-agnostic centroid. Operates on unnormalised embeddings.
𝒫y — YEAR
µy1 … µyY
One per year, shared across categories. Operates on ℓ₂-normalised residuals — temporal info purely in direction.
𝒦y — DISPLACEMENT
ky1 … kyY
Learnable temporal offsets for the swapping trick. Used only at training. Discarded at inference.
§3.2 · Proposition 1

Under Definition 1:

I(µc, µy; c, y) = I(µc; c) + I(µy; y)

The total information in the joint embedding decomposes into two independent sums — no cross-information between categorical and temporal components.

The proof is a two-line cancellation. Step through it — read the diagram as an information map: the total area is everything the proxies tell us about (category, year). Watch the cross-terms vanish, leaving two disjoint pools.

So v = f(x) is temporally decomposable: knowing both proxies captures everything v encodes about (c, y), with no shared (overlapping) information left between the temporal and categorical parts.

§3.2 · Lemma 1

Under Definition 1, the category and temporal centroids are orthogonal:

Cov(µc, µy) = 0    Var(µc + µy) = Var(µc) + Var(µy)

Independence → zero covariance → Pythagorean variance decomposition.

The two subspaces are perpendicular — drag the noise slider to see the effect of residual entanglement

⟨µc, µy⟩ = 0.00

As I(ε;c,y) grows, µc picks up a projection onto µy — but Theorem 1 shows joint optimisation drives this projection back to zero.

§3.2 · Proposition 2

In real data, residual noise ε entangles year and category. But:

I(v; c,y) ≤ I(µc; c) + I(µy; y) + δ   where  δ ≜ I(ε; c,y)

δ is a data-dependent constant — it does not depend on the proxy vectors µc, µy. The orthogonality constraint ⟨µc, µy⟩ = 0 can be satisfied without affecting this bound.

The error floor δ ignores how you orient the proxies — drag both knobs

An embedding splits as v = µc + µy + ε. You are free to rotate the orthogonal (µc, µy) frame to satisfy ⟨µc,µy⟩ = 0 — but that only re-shuffles information between the two proxies. The residual δ = I(ε; c,y) is fixed by the data alone, so the proxies can be learned freely without touching the bound.

Circularity resolved by Theorem 1: Proposition 2 assumes orthogonality to guarantee free learning — but orthogonality hasn't been enforced yet. Theorem 1 shows it emerges implicitly from the training strategy.
§3.4 · Theorem 1 (Emergent TDIR)

Part I (exact). If I(ε;c,y) = 0:

minθ ℒc + ℒy ⟹ p(µc, µy, c, y) = p(µc, c) · p(µy, y)

Part II (asymptotic). If I(ε;c,y) ≠ 0:

minθ ℒc + ℒy ⟹ ⟨µc, µy⟩ → O(I(ε;c,y) / ‖µy‖²) ∀c,y

Orthogonality is an emergent property of the joint optimisation — it does not need to be imposed explicitly.

§3.4 · Corollary 1
Projµy(µc) → 0    ∀ c, y

The shared displacement vectors ky cannot simultaneously compensate projections α ≠ α' across two categories — since ε is not a control variable, the unique globally consistent solution is α = α' = 0 for all c, c'.

The swapping trick forces residuals from different categories to be displaced by the same ky. This makes the optimizer implicitly require all category proxies to be orthogonal to all year proxies — not just category by category. It's the key to scalability.

What is α? Write each category centroid as µc = µ̃c + α·µyA + β·µyB. The coefficient α is simply how far µc leans into the year axis — the length of its shadow on µy. Orthogonality means α = 0 (no shadow). Below, α and α' are drawn as the coloured offsets that pull each residual off the year proxy.

Why one shared ky forces it globally — adjust each category's projection

Two categories (blue, red) must both have their year-A residual mapped onto the same target µyB by the same displacement kyB. If their projections α, α' differ, a single kyB cannot hit the target for both. The only globally consistent solution is α = α' = 0.

Chapter 6 · Conclusions

What TDIR Establishes

THEORETICAL
First formal treatment of temporal decomposability

Propositions 1 & 2 bound the decomposition error. Theorem 1 proves orthogonality emerges from joint optimisation. Corollary 1 shows the swapping trick enforces this cross-category.

EMPIRICAL
First zero-label composed historical retrieval

DEW extended with 7.2M detections across 50 categories. Image+Image mode transplants the year of one image onto the content of another — no labels required.

LIMITATIONS
Shared displacement; additive operator

A single temporal displacement across all categories may not reflect uneven historical evolution. Additive transplantation loses fidelity at large temporal distances.

FUTURE WORK
Non-linear operators; category-specific subspaces

Replace additive operator with a non-linear mechanism. Explore category-specific temporal subspaces. Extend to zero-shot evaluation over unseen categories.

The geometry of a well-structured embedding is not merely a tool for retrieval — it is a formal language for historical reasoning. Every µc + µy is a sentence: "Show me this object from this era."
